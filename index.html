A Stronger Atlanta Starts in Every Neighborhood

The Neighborhood Reinvestment Initiative invests in areas that have experienced decades of disinvestment — strengthening communities, growing the tax base, and benefiting the entire city.

How a Tax Allocation District Works

A TAD reinvests local growth back into the neighborhood where it happens.

1. Neighborhood Needs Investment

Some parts of Atlanta have gone decades without the infrastructure, housing, and services needed to thrive.

2. Growth Funds Improvements

As development happens, property values increase. TADs capture that growth and reinvest it locally.

3. The Whole City Benefits

Stronger neighborhoods increase Atlanta’s overall tax base — supporting schools, infrastructure, and services.

TAD extensions do not raise taxes. They reinvest growth created within a district.

Why This Matters

Not every part of Atlanta has shared equally in the city’s growth. Decades of uneven investment have left gaps in infrastructure, housing, and opportunity. Addressing those gaps strengthens the entire city.

Common Questions

Does this raise taxes?

No. TADs reinvest growth in property values — they do not increase tax rates.

Does this hurt schools?

Over time, TADs expand the tax base, increasing long-term funding for schools.

What about displacement?

NRI includes strategies to help residents stay in their communities as investment occurs.

Is this a slush fund?

Projects are reviewed and approved through public processes with oversight and accountability.

Why now?

Atlanta’s growth creates an opportunity to invest in areas that have historically been left behind.
